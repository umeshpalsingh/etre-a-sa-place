The Book

Being in His Place: Inhabiting Your Life, Inhabiting Your Body

“Sometimes it starts with worry or discomfort. We feel out of step, we are afraid of acting inappropriately. We have the feeling of not "being in his place". But what is it to be in his place, in his family, his couple, his work? What are the spaces, real or symbolic, that welcome or reject us? Should we try to conquer the places that are forbidden to us, because of our gender, our disability, our age, our ethnic or social origin? Perhaps we should transform these places from the inside and create a place for ourselves? In this fascinating and sensitive work, the philosopher Claire Marin explores all the places we occupy - daily, voluntarily or against our will, those we have lost, those we fear losing - and questions what is both the formulation of a personal desire and a new social imperative. It remains to be seen if we all end up finding a place, or if the characteristic of a place is not rather to constantly move, or to move those who believe they can settle there. ..
